Archeops - SWSH12 147/195

Archeops is the main card in the deck. The ability is extremely powerful allowing you to break a rule of the game by attaching more than 1 energy per turn. It won't be used as an attacker very much, but it's not a horrible option..

LugiaV - SWSH12 138/195

Lugia V is essential to the deck, but ultimately useless in the grand scheme of things. It's necessary for getting out your Lugia VStar, but you'll likely never attack with it. However, it has a decent attack called "Read the Wind" that helps you set up early on.

LugiaV - SWSH12 068/195

Lugia VStar isn't so much an attacker as it is more of a mandatory setup Pokemon. It has the powerful VStar ability to bring out your Archeops, which the deck relies on. Don't underestimate it's usefulness as an attacker since it boasts high HP and a fairly good attack!

TyranitarV - SWSH10 097/163

Tyranitar V is the main attacker in the deck as it can hit extremely high damage for massive knockouts. It uses a combination of Single Strike Energy and the other energy in your deck to power up the massive attack.

Yveltal - CLB 175/264

Yveltal is one of the attacker options in a Lugia deck. It utilizes Single Strike Energy to power up it's attacks, which boost it's damage. Choose to use it against Mew and Gardevoir as that's when it's the strongest.
